Thalassophile

Preserving our ocean is very important because a healthy ocean regulates climate and mitigates the effects of climate change. Ocean currents transport heat around the world, regulating temperature and weather. The ocean also absorbs more than 90% of the heat and approximately 30% of the carbon dioxide emissions produced by human activity.

Human activity endangers marine life. We can save the ocean with your help. We can prevent issues like vessel strikes, chemical pollution, and marine debris by working together.

Beach

1. White Beach, Boracay

Boracay, located south of Manila in the Aklan province, is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the Philippines.

The beach is absolutely stunning, earning its name from its blindingly white, powdery sand that dips into crystal clear waters. When you see it, you'll understand why it's so popular right away.

2. Coron Palawan

Coron appears to be some otherworldly, modern-day fairy-tale-like area perfect for that escape to unwind and soak up the Philippines natural beauty, with its steep limestone cliffs plunging into the blue water.

Coron is mentioned as a larger area than just its specific beaches because there are so many beautiful beaches here that you'll be spoiled for choice. This area of the Philippines is one of the best places in the world to scuba dive, which explains why it has some of the best beaches in the country.
